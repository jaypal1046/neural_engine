Question: What is the scientific method?

Ideal Answer: The scientific method is the systematic process for acquiring reliable knowledge through observation and testing.

**Steps:**
1. **Observe** — notice a phenomenon
2. **Question** — formulate a specific question
3. **Hypothesis** — propose a testable explanation
4. **Experiment** — design a controlled test (change one variable, keep others constant)
5. **Analyze** — collect and interpret data
6. **Conclude** — does data support or refute hypothesis?
7. **Replicate** — others try to reproduce results
8. **Publish** — peer review by other scientists

**Key principle — falsifiability (Popper):** A good scientific hypothesis must be possible to disprove. 'All swans are white' is scientific — finding a black swan falsifies it. Unfalsifiable claims are not science.

The scientific method has driven more human progress than any other epistemological tool.
