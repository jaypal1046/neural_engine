Question: What is the difference between weather and climate?

Ideal Answer: **Weather** = short-term atmosphere conditions at a specific place and time.
- 'It's raining in Mumbai today'
- Changes hour to hour, day to day
- Measured by: temperature, humidity, wind, precipitation

**Climate** = long-term patterns of weather over decades in a region.
- 'Mumbai has a monsoon season from June to September'
- Defined over 30+ year averages (WMO standard)
- Measured by: seasonal averages, extreme event frequency

**Analogy:** Weather is your mood. Climate is your personality.

**Climate change context:**
- Burning fossil fuels increases atmospheric CO₂
- CO₂ traps heat (greenhouse effect)
- Global average temperature has risen ~1.2°C since pre-industrial times
- This shifts climate patterns globally — more extreme weather events, rising sea levels

Skills demonstrated: clear distinction, analogy, climate change connection
