Question: How do I think about tradeoffs?

Ideal Answer: Every decision involves tradeoffs — gaining one thing at the cost of another. Clear tradeoff thinking:

1. **Identify the tradeoffs explicitly** — what do you gain? What do you give up?
2. **Understand your constraints** — time, money, correctness, simplicity?
3. **Think in reversibility** — prefer reversible decisions when uncertain. Hard to reverse = extra caution.
4. **Consider the 2nd order effects** — what happens AFTER the first consequence?
5. **Check your biases** — are you drawn to the option that feels familiar or comfortable?

**Classic CS tradeoffs:**
- Speed vs memory (cache more, compute less OR compute more, store less)
- Consistency vs availability (CAP theorem)
- Simplicity vs flexibility (hard-coding vs config files)
- Accuracy vs speed (approximate algorithms)

**Jeff Bezos rule:** Prioritize being 'roughly right quickly' over 'precisely right slowly' for reversible decisions.
