Question: How does the immune system work?

Ideal Answer: The immune system is the body's defense network against pathogens (bacteria, viruses, fungi, parasites).

**Two layers:**

**1. Innate immunity (fast, non-specific):**
- Physical barriers: skin, mucus, stomach acid
- White blood cells (neutrophils, macrophages) engulf and destroy invaders
- Inflammation: redness/swelling signals that repair is happening
- Response time: minutes to hours

**2. Adaptive immunity (slow, highly specific, lasting):**
- **B cells** produce antibodies — proteins that bind to specific pathogens and neutralize them
- **T cells** kill infected cells directly or coordinate immune response
- **Memory cells** persist for years — why vaccines and past infections provide immunity
- Response time: days to weeks (first exposure); hours (re-exposure, due to memory)

**Vaccines** work by exposing immune system to harmless pathogen fragment → generates memory cells → future infection is defeated quickly.
