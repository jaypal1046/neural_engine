Question: What is inflation?

Ideal Answer: Inflation is the general rise in the price level of goods and services over time, meaning each unit of currency buys fewer goods.

**Measured by:** CPI (Consumer Price Index) — tracks basket of typical purchases over time.

**Types:**
- **Demand-pull:** too much money chasing too few goods (post-COVID stimulus)
- **Cost-push:** production costs rise (oil price shock → everything gets pricier)
- **Built-in:** workers demand higher wages → companies raise prices → cycle continues

**Effects:**
- Erodes savings if returns < inflation rate
- Helps debtors (pay back with cheaper money)
- Hurts fixed-income earners
- Hyperinflation (>50%/month) destroys economies (Zimbabwe 2008, Weimar Germany)

**Central bank response:** Raise interest rates → borrowing costs more → spending falls → inflation cools.

Skills demonstrated: measurement, 3 types, effects, central bank response
