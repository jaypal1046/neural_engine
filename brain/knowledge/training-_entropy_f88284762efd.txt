Question: What is entropy?

Ideal Answer: Entropy (in information theory) measures how random or unpredictable data is. Claude Shannon defined it as:

`H = -Σ p(x) * log₂(p(x))`

In practice:
- **8.0 bits/byte** = perfectly random (encrypted files, already compressed) — cannot be compressed further
- **4.0–7.0 bits** = structured data (text, code) — compresses well
- **< 2.0 bits** = highly repetitive — compresses extremely well

Low entropy = predictable = compressible. The CMIX engine uses 1,046 models to minimize prediction error, achieving near-theoretical compression.
