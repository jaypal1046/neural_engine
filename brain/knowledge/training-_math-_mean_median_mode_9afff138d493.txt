Question: What is the difference between mean, median, and mode?

Ideal Answer: These are three measures of central tendency in statistics:

- **Mean** = sum of all values ÷ count. Sensitive to outliers. `[1,2,3,4,100]` → mean = 22
- **Median** = the middle value when sorted. Robust to outliers. `[1,2,3,4,100]` → median = 3
- **Mode** = the most frequent value. `[1,2,2,3,4]` → mode = 2

**When to use:**
- Mean: symmetric data, no extreme outliers (exam scores)
- Median: skewed data, outliers present (income, house prices)
- Mode: categorical data (most popular color)
