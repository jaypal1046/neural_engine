Question: What is Newton's second law?

Ideal Answer: Newton's second law: **Force = mass × acceleration**

`F = ma`

- F = net force (Newtons, N)
- m = mass (kilograms, kg)
- a = acceleration (m/s²)

**What this means:**
- Larger force → more acceleration
- Larger mass → less acceleration from same force
- Direction matters — F and a are vectors

**Example:** A 10 kg box pushed with 20 N of force accelerates at 2 m/s². If you push twice as hard (40 N), it accelerates at 4 m/s².

This is the central law of classical mechanics, connecting cause (force) to effect (acceleration).
