Question: What is a logical fallacy?

Ideal Answer: A logical fallacy is an error in reasoning that makes an argument invalid even if its conclusion might happen to be true.

**Common fallacies:**
- **Ad hominem** — attack the person, not the argument ('You can't trust his climate data — he's unpopular')
- **Straw man** — misrepresent opponent's position to more easily attack it
- **False dichotomy** — present only 2 options when more exist ('You're either with us or against us')
- **Appeal to authority** — 'X is true because famous person Y said so'
- **Slippery slope** — if A happens, then Z will inevitably follow (without showing steps)
- **Post hoc** — 'A happened before B, therefore A caused B' (classic correlation ≠ causation)
- **Hasty generalization** — draw broad conclusion from small sample

**Why it matters:** Recognizing fallacies protects you from bad arguments in politics, advertising, and everyday debate.
