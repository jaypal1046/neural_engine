Question: How does music affect the brain?

Ideal Answer: Music activates more brain regions simultaneously than almost any other stimulus:

**Neurological effects:**
- **Dopamine release** — pleasurable music triggers same reward circuits as food and sex (explains chills/'frisson')
- **Motor cortex activation** — music with a beat automatically activates movement planning (why we tap feet)
- **Memory** — music is processed in hippocampus alongside autobiographical memories (why old songs bring vivid memories)
- **Emotion** — amygdala responds to musical tension and resolution

**Practical applications:**
- **Pain management** — music reduces perceived pain intensity and anxiety in medical procedures
- **Stroke recovery** — singing-based therapy helps speech recovery (left vs right brain hemispheres for language/music)
- **Focus/productivity** — ~120 BPM background music slightly improves concentration on repetitive tasks
- **Alzheimer's** — patients who can no longer recognize family still respond emotionally to familiar music (music memory is highly durable)

**Mozart Effect** is largely a myth — one study, poorly replicated. Being a musician is far more beneficial.
