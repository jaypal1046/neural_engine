Question: What is DNA?

Ideal Answer: DNA (Deoxyribonucleic Acid) is the molecule that stores the genetic blueprint for all living organisms.

**Structure:**
- Double helix — two strands twisted together (Watson & Crick, 1953)
- 4 nitrogen bases: Adenine (A), Thymine (T), Cytosine (C), Guanine (G)
- Bases pair: A–T and C–G (complementary base pairing)
- A sequence of 3 bases (codon) encodes one amino acid

**Central dogma of biology:**
DNA → RNA → Protein
- DNA is transcribed into messenger RNA
- mRNA is translated into proteins by ribosomes
- Proteins do virtually everything in the cell

**Human genome:** ~3.2 billion base pairs encoding ~20,000 genes, stored in 23 chromosome pairs.
