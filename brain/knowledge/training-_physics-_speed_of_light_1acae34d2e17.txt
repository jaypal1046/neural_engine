Question: What is the speed of light?

Ideal Answer: The speed of light in a vacuum is exactly **299,792,458 meters per second** (≈ 3 × 10⁸ m/s, or ~186,000 miles/second).

**Why c matters:**
- Einstein's special relativity: nothing with mass can reach c
- c is the cosmic speed limit
- E = mc² — mass and energy are interchangeable
- Light from the Sun takes ~8 minutes to reach Earth
- Light from the nearest star (Proxima Centauri) takes 4.24 years

**If c were slower:** GPS satellites would give wrong positions (relativity corrections would be larger). The universe as we know it would be completely different.
