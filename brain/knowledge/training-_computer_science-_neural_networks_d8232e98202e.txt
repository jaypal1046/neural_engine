Question: What is a neural network?

Ideal Answer: A neural network refers to a computational model inspired by the human brain, consisting of layers of interconnected nodes (neurons).

1. Input layer: receives raw data (pixels, words, numbers).
2. Hidden layers: transform the data through weighted connections and activation functions (e.g., ReLU, sigmoid).
3. Output layer: produces the final prediction (class, value, token).
4. Training: the network adjusts weights via backpropagation to minimize prediction error.

Deep neural networks (many hidden layers) power image recognition (CNN), language models (Transformer), and game-playing AI (deep RL). Key concept: each weight represents the strength of a connection learned from data.

Skills demonstrated: architecture, layers, training, activation functions, applications
