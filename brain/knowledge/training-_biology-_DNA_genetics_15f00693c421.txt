Question: What is DNA?

Ideal Answer: DNA (deoxyribonucleic acid) refers to the molecule that carries genetic information in all living organisms.

1. DNA is a double helix — two strands twisted around each other, connected by base pairs.
2. The four bases are Adenine (A), Thymine (T), Guanine (G), and Cytosine (C). A pairs with T; G pairs with C.
3. Genes are segments of DNA that encode proteins, which carry out cellular functions.
4. Humans have about 3 billion base pairs in 23 chromosome pairs.

DNA replication copies the genome before cell division. Mutations in DNA can cause disease or drive evolution. The Human Genome Project mapped all human DNA by 2003.
