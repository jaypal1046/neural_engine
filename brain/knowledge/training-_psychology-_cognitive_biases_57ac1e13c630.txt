Question: What is cognitive bias?

Ideal Answer: A cognitive bias is a systematic error in thinking that affects judgments and decisions. These biases evolved as mental shortcuts (heuristics) but lead to predictable mistakes.

**Important biases:**
- **Confirmation bias** — seek/interpret info that confirms existing beliefs
- **Dunning-Kruger effect** — low-skill people overestimate competence; experts underestimate knowledge
- **Anchoring** — first number seen biases all subsequent judgments
- **Availability heuristic** — overestimate likelihood of memorable/recent events (plane crash fear > car crash)
- **Sunk cost fallacy** — continue bad decision because of past investment
- **Survivorship bias** — only seeing successes, not failures (startup advice)

**Why it matters:** Understanding biases improves decision-making in medicine, business, law, and everyday life.
