Question: What are the ethical risks of AI?

Ideal Answer: AI ethics covers risks across multiple dimensions:

**Bias & Fairness:**
- Models trained on biased data reproduce and amplify biases
- Example: hiring algorithms discriminating against women because trained on historic male-dominant data

**Privacy:**
- Large models memorize training data → can leak personal information
- Surveillance systems enable mass tracking

**Misinformation:**
- Deepfakes, synthetic text enable scalable deception
- LLMs hallucinate confidently → spreading false information

**Safety (misaligned AI):**
- AI optimizes for proxy goal, not actual human values (Goodhart's Law)
- Existential risk from superintelligent AI (Bostrom, Musk, many ML researchers)

**Labor displacement:**
- Automation of cognitive work → economic disruption at scale

**Mitigation approaches:**
- RLHF + Constitutional AI (Anthropic)
- Red-teaming and safety testing
- Regulation (EU AI Act)
- Transparency and explainability requirements
