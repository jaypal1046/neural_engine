Question: What caused World War 1?

Ideal Answer: WWI (1914–1918) was caused by a combination of long-term tensions and a specific trigger:

**MAIN acronym (long-term causes):**
- **M**ilitarism — European powers built up massive militaries
- **A**lliances — Triple Entente (UK, France, Russia) vs Triple Alliance (Germany, Austria, Italy)
- **I**mperialism — competition for colonies created friction
- **N**ationalism — rising ethnic independence movements, especially in the Balkans

**Trigger:** Assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand (heir to Austria-Hungary) by Gavrilo Princip in Sarajevo (June 28, 1914)

**Chain reaction:** Austria declared war on Serbia → Russia mobilized → Germany declared war on Russia and France → Germany invaded Belgium → Britain declared war

**Result:** ~20 million deaths, collapse of 4 empires (Ottoman, Austro-Hungarian, Russian, German).

Skills demonstrated: MAIN acronym, trigger event, cascade explained, result
