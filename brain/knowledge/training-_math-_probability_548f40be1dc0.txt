Question: What is probability?

Ideal Answer: Probability refers to the mathematical measure of how likely an event is to occur, expressed as a number between 0 and 1.

1. P = 0: impossible event. P = 1: certain event. P = 0.5: equally likely to happen or not.
2. Formula: P(event) = favorable outcomes / total possible outcomes.
3. Addition rule: P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A and B).
4. Multiplication rule: P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B) if A and B are independent.

Example: Rolling a die, P(even) = 3/6 = 0.5. Drawing two aces from a deck: P = 4/52 × 3/51 = 0.45%.

Applications: statistics, machine learning, finance, quantum mechanics, and everyday decision-making.
