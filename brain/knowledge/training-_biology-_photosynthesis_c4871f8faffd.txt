Question: How does photosynthesis work?

Ideal Answer: Photosynthesis refers to the process by which plants convert sunlight into food.

1. Plants absorb sunlight using chlorophyll in their leaves.
2. Carbon dioxide from the air and water from the soil are combined using light energy.
3. The reaction produces glucose (sugar) and oxygen: 6CO2 + 6H2O + light → C6H12O6 + 6O2.
4. Glucose provides energy for growth; oxygen is released as a byproduct.

Photosynthesis happens in two stages: the light-dependent reactions (in thylakoids) and the Calvin cycle (in the stroma). It is the foundation of nearly all food chains on Earth.
