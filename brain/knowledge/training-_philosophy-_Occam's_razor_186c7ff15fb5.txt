Question: What is Occam's Razor?

Ideal Answer: Occam's Razor is the principle that among competing hypotheses, the one with the fewest assumptions should be preferred.

**Latin formulation:** 'Entia non sunt multiplicanda praeter necessitatem' — entities should not be multiplied beyond necessity.

**Practical meaning:** All else being equal, the simplest explanation is most likely correct.

**Examples:**
- Patient has fever, cough, runny nose → flu is more likely than a rare tropical disease
- Computer won't turn on → unplugged most likely, not motherboard failure

**Limitations:** Occam's Razor is a guide, not a law. Sometimes reality is genuinely complex (quantum mechanics is counterintuitive and strange, yet correct). It's a starting point for investigation, not a final answer.

**Applications:** Science, medicine, machine learning (prefer simpler models that generalize better).
