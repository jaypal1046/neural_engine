Question: What is pH?

Ideal Answer: pH measures the acidity or alkalinity of a solution, on a scale from 0 to 14:

- **0–6:** Acidic (more H⁺ ions than OH⁻)
- **7:** Neutral (pure water)
- **8–14:** Basic/Alkaline (more OH⁻ ions)

**Formula:** `pH = -log₁₀[H⁺]`

Each step is 10× more acidic/basic:
- pH 5 is **10× more acidic** than pH 6
- pH 3 (vinegar) is **10,000× more acidic** than pH 7 (water)

**Common pH values:**
- Battery acid: ~1 | Lemon juice: ~2 | Vinegar: ~3 | Black coffee: ~5
- Blood: 7.4 | Baking soda: 9 | Bleach: ~12 | Drain cleaner: ~14
