Question: What is inflation?

Ideal Answer: Inflation refers to the general increase in prices across an economy over time, reducing the purchasing power of money.

1. Measured by CPI (Consumer Price Index) — tracks price changes in a basket of goods.
2. Demand-pull inflation: too much money chasing too few goods (economy overheating).
3. Cost-push inflation: production costs rise (e.g., oil prices) → prices increase.
4. Hyperinflation: extreme rapid inflation (e.g., Zimbabwe 2008, Germany 1923) destroys savings.

Central banks (e.g., Federal Reserve) fight inflation by raising interest rates, which reduces borrowing and spending. Target inflation: 2% per year in most developed economies.
