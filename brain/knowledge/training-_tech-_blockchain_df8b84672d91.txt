Question: What is blockchain?

Ideal Answer: A blockchain is a distributed, append-only ledger where data is stored in cryptographically linked blocks.

**How it works:**
1. Transactions are grouped into a **block**
2. The block includes a **hash** of the previous block (creating the 'chain')
3. Multiple nodes validate new blocks (consensus mechanism)
4. Once added, blocks cannot be altered without re-doing all subsequent hashes (tamper-evident)

**Key properties:**
- **Decentralized** — no single authority controls it
- **Transparent** — anyone can read (in public chains like Bitcoin)
- **Immutable** — changing history requires consensus of majority of nodes

**Use cases:**
- Cryptocurrency (Bitcoin, Ethereum)
- Smart contracts (self-executing code on Ethereum)
- Supply chain tracking (who handled this food/product at each step)

**Limitations:** Slow (7 tx/sec for Bitcoin vs 65,000 for Visa), high energy use (proof-of-work), often not needed when a trusted database would suffice.
