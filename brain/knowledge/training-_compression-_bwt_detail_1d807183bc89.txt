Question: What is BWT?

Ideal Answer: BWT (Burrows-Wheeler Transform) is a reversible text rearrangement that clusters similar bytes together — making downstream compression far easier.

**Example with 'BANANA':**
1. Generate all cyclic rotations and sort them alphabetically
2. Take the last column: → `NNBAAA` (similar letters clump together)
3. This 'run-length-friendly' output is then encoded with MTF + RLE + ANS

BWT is lossless — the original is perfectly reconstructed using the primary index. In our engine it powers the `--best` mode, achieving 55–70% ratio on text files with fast speed.
