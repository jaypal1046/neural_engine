Question: How big is the universe?

Ideal Answer: The observable universe is approximately **93 billion light-years in diameter** — this is the sphere we can, in principle, observe from Earth.

**Key scales:**
- Earth → Moon: 1.3 light-seconds
- Earth → Sun: 8 light-minutes
- Solar system edge (Oort cloud): ~1 light-year
- Nearest star (Proxima Centauri): 4.24 light-years
- Milky Way diameter: ~100,000 light-years (~200–400 billion stars)
- Nearest galaxy (Andromeda): 2.537 million light-years
- Observable universe: ~93 billion light-years
- Estimated 2 trillion galaxies in the observable universe

**The universe may be infinite** — we can only observe as far as light has traveled since the Big Bang (~13.8 billion years ago). The actual universe could be vastly larger than the observable portion.
