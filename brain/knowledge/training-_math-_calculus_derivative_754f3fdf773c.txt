Question: What is a derivative?

Ideal Answer: A derivative measures the instantaneous rate of change of a function. Formally:

`f'(x) = lim(h→0) [f(x+h) - f(x)] / h`

Intuitively: the slope of the tangent line to the curve at point x.

**Key rules:**
- Power rule: d/dx(xⁿ) = n·xⁿ⁻¹
- Product rule: d/dx(f·g) = f'g + fg'
- Chain rule: d/dx(f(g(x))) = f'(g(x))·g'(x)

**Example:** d/dx(x³) = 3x². At x=2, the slope is 3×4 = 12.
