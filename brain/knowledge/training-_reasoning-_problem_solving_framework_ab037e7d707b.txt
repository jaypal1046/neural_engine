Question: How do I solve a problem I've never seen before?

Ideal Answer: A systematic problem-solving framework (used by both engineers and scientists):

1. **Understand the problem** — can you restate it in your own words? What are the inputs, outputs, constraints?
2. **Explore examples** — work through small concrete cases by hand. This reveals patterns.
3. **Break it down** — decompose into sub-problems. Which parts can you solve already?
4. **Identify knowns** — what tools, algorithms, or patterns apply? (sorting, recursion, dynamic programming?)
5. **Draft a solution** — write pseudocode before real code. Get the structure right first.
6. **Implement and test** — build it, test edge cases (empty input, maximum input, duplicates)
7. **Reflect and optimize** — does it work? Is there a simpler or faster approach?

**Key mindset:** When stuck, add more examples or simplify the problem (solve for n=1 first, then generalize).
