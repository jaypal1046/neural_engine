Question: What is the difference between lossless and lossy compression?

Ideal Answer: **Lossless compression:** Original data is perfectly reconstructed from the compressed version. Not a single bit is changed.
- Examples: ZIP, PNG, FLAC, our CMIX/BWT engine (.aiz)
- Used for: documents, source code, databases, RAW images (where perfection matters)

**Lossy compression:** Some data is permanently discarded — the decompressed file is an approximation.
- Examples: JPEG (images), MP3/AAC (audio), H.264 (video)
- Used for: media where human perception can't notice small losses
- JPEG works by discarding high-frequency detail in 8×8 pixel blocks — eyes barely notice

**Comparison:**
| | Lossless | Lossy |
|---|---|---|
| Reconstruction | Perfect | Approximate |
| Ratio (text) | 25–80% | N/A (text must be lossless) |
| Ratio (images) | 2:1 | 10:1 to 30:1 |
| Multiple compress | Safe | Degrades (generation loss) |

**Our engine** is 100% lossless — every decompressed .aiz file is bit-for-bit identical to the original.
