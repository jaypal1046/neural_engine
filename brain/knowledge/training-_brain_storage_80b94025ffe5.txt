Question: How does your brain store knowledge?

Ideal Answer: Every piece of knowledge goes through this pipeline:
1. **Tokenize** — split text into clean lowercase tokens, remove stop words
2. **Extract keywords** — top 25 words by frequency (TF-IDF weighted)
3. **Summarize** — extract 4 most important sentences (keyword density scoring)
4. **Compress** — store it with CMIX neural compression (typically 40–80% savings)
5. **Index** — update the brain_index.json with keywords, summary, and metadata

Retrieval uses TF-IDF similarity scoring against stored documents. The C++ neural engine adds semantic similarity re-ranking.
