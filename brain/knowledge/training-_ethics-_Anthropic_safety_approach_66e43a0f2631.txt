Question: What is Anthropic's approach to AI safety?

Ideal Answer: Anthropic was founded in 2021 by ex-OpenAI researchers focused specifically on AI safety. Their approach includes:

**1. Constitutional AI (CAI):**
Train Claude to follow a written set of principles using self-critique — the AI checks its own outputs against the 'constitution' without requiring human feedback on every example.

**2. RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback):**
Human labelers rank AI responses → train a reward model → use that to optimize Claude's outputs.

**3. Interpretability research:**
Understanding *what's happening inside* neural networks — which circuits activate for which concepts. Goal: detect dangerous behaviors before deployment.

**4. Scaling with safety:**
Harmlessness, helpfulness, and honesty ('HHH') as core objectives — not just capability.

**5. Gradual deployment:**
Test with limited users first, expand only after safety validation.

Anthropic publishes safety research papers and positions itself as a 'safety-first' commercial AI lab.
