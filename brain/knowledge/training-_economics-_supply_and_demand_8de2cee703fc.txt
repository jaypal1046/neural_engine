Question: What is supply and demand?

Ideal Answer: Supply and demand is the fundamental mechanism that determines prices in a free market.

**Demand:** As price rises, quantity demanded falls (inverse relationship). Demand shifts right if: income rises, population grows, preferences change.

**Supply:** As price rises, quantity supplied rises (direct relationship). Supply shifts right if: production costs fall, technology improves.

**Equilibrium:** The price where supply = demand. At this price, the market 'clears' — no surplus, no shortage.

**Key shifts:**
- Demand surges (COVID hand sanitizer) → price rises until new equilibrium
- Supply crashes (drought destroying wheat crop) → price rises
- New technology (solar panels) → supply increases → price falls

**Elasticity:** How sensitive demand/supply is to price changes. Gasoline = inelastic (you still buy it). Luxury goods = elastic (price hike kills demand).
