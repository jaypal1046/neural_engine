Question: What is LZ77?

Ideal Answer: LZ77 is a dictionary-based compression algorithm that replaces repeated sequences with (distance, length) back-references.

**How it works:**
1. Scan the input with a sliding window (up to 32KB in our engine)
2. When a repeated sequence is found, emit a token: `(distance_back, match_length)`
3. Unique bytes are emitted as literals
4. Our engine uses 'lazy matching' (zlib level 4+ strategy) for better ratios

LZ77 is the **default** algorithm — fast, low memory, good for general files. For text & code, `--best` (BWT) or `--cmix` will beat it significantly.
