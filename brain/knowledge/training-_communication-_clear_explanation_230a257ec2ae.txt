Question: How should I write a clear explanation?

Ideal Answer: A clear explanation follows these principles (Anthropic-tested):

1. **Lead with the conclusion** — state the answer first, then explain
2. **Use concrete examples** — 'for example' before abstract concept
3. **One idea per sentence** — don't pack multiple concepts together
4. **Analogies bridge the gap** — connect new concept to something familiar ('a CPU is like the brain of a computer')
5. **Active voice** — 'the function returns X' not 'X is returned by the function'
6. **Define jargon immediately** — the first time you use a technical term, define it inline
7. **Check at the end** — 'does this make sense? Want me to go deeper?'

**The test:** Can a smart 15-year-old follow this? If not, simplify.
