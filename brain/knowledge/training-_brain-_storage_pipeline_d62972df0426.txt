Question: How does your brain store knowledge?

Ideal Answer: Every knowledge item goes through this exact pipeline:
1. **Tokenize** — split into lowercase tokens, remove 100+ stop words
2. **Extract keywords** — top 25 by frequency (TF-IDF weighted against all docs)
3. **Summarize** — 4 most important sentences by keyword density
4. **Compress** — CMIX neural compression (typically 25–80% savings)
5. **Index** — stored in `brain_index.json` with keywords, summary, metadata

All stored compressed as `.aiz` archives in `brain/knowledge/`. Retrieval uses TF-IDF scoring + optional C++ neural semantic similarity re-ranking.
