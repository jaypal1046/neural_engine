Question: What is the difference between a virus and a bacterium?

Ideal Answer: Both cause infections, but they are fundamentally different:

| Feature | Bacteria | Virus |
|---------|---------|-------|
| Size | 1–10 μm | 0.01–0.3 μm (10-100x smaller) |
| Structure | Single cell with cell wall | Protein coat + genetic material (DNA or RNA) |
| Living? | Yes — can reproduce alone | No — needs host cell to replicate |
| Treatment | **Antibiotics** (kill bacteria) | **Antivirals** / **Vaccines** |
| Examples | Strep throat, TB, E. coli | Flu, COVID, HIV, common cold |

**Key insight:** Antibiotics CANNOT treat viral infections. Overusing antibiotics for viruses is a major cause of antibiotic resistance — one of the biggest public health threats.
