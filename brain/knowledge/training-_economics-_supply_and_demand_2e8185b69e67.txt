Question: What is supply and demand?

Ideal Answer: Supply and demand refers to the fundamental economic model describing how prices and quantities are determined in a market.

1. Demand: the quantity buyers want at each price — higher prices → lower demand (inverse relationship).
2. Supply: the quantity sellers offer at each price — higher prices → higher supply (direct relationship).
3. Equilibrium: the price where quantity supplied equals quantity demanded — no surplus or shortage.
4. Shifts: demand shifts when income, preferences, or related-good prices change. Supply shifts with input costs, technology, or regulations.

Example: When smartphones became popular, demand for data plans surged → prices rose until new providers entered and supply increased, stabilizing prices.
