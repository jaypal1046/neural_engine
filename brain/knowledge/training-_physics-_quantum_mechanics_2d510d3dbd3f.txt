Question: What is quantum mechanics?

Ideal Answer: Quantum mechanics describes the behavior of matter and energy at atomic and subatomic scales, where classical physics breaks down.

**Key principles:**
1. **Wave-particle duality** — particles (electrons, photons) behave as both waves and particles depending on observation
2. **Uncertainty principle** — you cannot simultaneously know exact position AND momentum of a particle (Heisenberg: ΔxΔp ≥ ℏ/2)
3. **Quantization** — energy comes in discrete packets called quanta (photons)
4. **Superposition** — a particle can be in multiple states simultaneously until measured (Schrödinger's cat paradox)
5. **Entanglement** — two particles can be correlated such that measuring one instantly affects the other

Quantum mechanics underlies lasers, transistors, MRI machines, and quantum computers.
