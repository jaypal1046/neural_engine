Question: What is Maslow's hierarchy of needs?

Ideal Answer: Maslow's hierarchy (1943) is a motivational theory proposing that human needs are arranged in priority order — lower needs must be met before higher ones become motivating.

**5 levels (bottom to top):**
1. **Physiological** — food, water, shelter, sleep, warmth
2. **Safety** — personal security, employment, health, property
3. **Love/Belonging** — friendship, intimacy, family, community
4. **Esteem** — self-respect, achievement, recognition, status
5. **Self-Actualization** — reaching full potential, creativity, purpose

**Applications:** Management theory (employees need safety before they can focus on achievement), therapy, product design (Airbnb → safety+belonging before luxury).

**Criticism:** The strict hierarchy doesn't hold universally — culture affects which needs drive motivation most.
