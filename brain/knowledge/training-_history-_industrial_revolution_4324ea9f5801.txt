Question: What was the Industrial Revolution?

Ideal Answer: The Industrial Revolution (roughly 1760–1840, starting in Britain) was the transition from hand production to machine-based manufacturing, fundamentally reshaping human society.

**Key innovations:**
- James Watt's steam engine (1769) — powered factories, trains, ships
- Spinning jenny, power loom — mechanized textile production
- Iron/steel production grew massively
- Railways — first modern high-speed transport network

**Effects:**
- Urbanization — people moved from farms to factory cities
- New social classes: industrial capitalists and urban working class
- Child labor and poor factory conditions → labor reform movements
- GDP grew faster than any time in human history before it
- Began modern economic growth but also carbon emissions

Spread from Britain to Europe, USA, and Japan by 1900.
