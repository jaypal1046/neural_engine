Question: What is the Big Bang?

Ideal Answer: The Big Bang is the leading cosmological model explaining the origin and evolution of the universe — it describes the universe expanding from an extremely hot, dense state approximately **13.8 billion years ago**.

**Key evidence:**
1. **Hubble expansion** — galaxies are moving away from us; farther = faster. Reverse time → everything was together.
2. **Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB)** — faint glow of radiation filling all of space, remnant 'echo' of the hot early universe (discovered 1965)
3. **Abundance of light elements** — measured ratios of hydrogen, helium, lithium match Big Bang nucleosynthesis predictions precisely

**Timeline:**
- t=0: Singularity (all energy in infinite density)
- t=10⁻³² s: Inflation (rapid expansion)
- t=3 minutes: Protons/neutrons form first nuclei
- t=380,000 years: Universe cools enough for atoms to form (CMB released)
- t=200 million years: First stars ignite
